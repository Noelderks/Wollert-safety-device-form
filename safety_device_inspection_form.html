Safety Device Inspection Form

Emergency Stop Lanyard

Inspection Criteria: Emergency stop lanyards require full coverage of hazardous zones, proper tension maintained by end-positioned springs, and immediate stop functionality. They must be accessible, durable, and manually resettable, with red rope and clear signage. Lanyards should be fail-safe and resistant to wear and environmental conditions.

Compliance References: SWA WHS Regulation 191 VIC OHS Regulations r79 (1) and (2) and AS 4024 standards. and Plant Safety Requirements No. 1 ~ SHE-MSP-WHS-07.013

Emergency Stop Button

Inspection Criteria: Emergency stop buttons must be inspected routinely to ensure they are clearly visible, easily accessible, and functioning correctly. They must immediately stop machinery, be manually resettable, and operate independently of electrical faults. The actuator must be red, with Yellow background .

Compliance References: SWA WHS Regulation 191 VIC OHS Regulations r79 (1) and (2) and AS 4024 standards. and Plant Safety Requirements No. 1 ~ SHE-MSP-WHS-07.013

Interlocked Safety Gate Inspection

Inspection Criteria: Interlocked safety gates must be inspected regularly to ensure structural integrity, secure mounting, and reliable interlock function. The gate must prevent access during machine operation and block machine start-up when open. Electrical systems must be intact and fail-safe.

Machine Isolator Inspection

Inspection Criteria: Machine isolators must be securely mounted, and fully functional. They must completely disconnect energy sources, be lockable in the OFF position, and prevent accidental reactivation. Wiring and labels must be intact, and isolation points tested routinely.

Interlocked Key System Inspection

Inspection Criteria: Interlocked key systems must be secure, undamaged, and functioning correctly. The system must enforce a safe sequence, preventing access to hazardous areas unless isolation is confirmed. Keys should only be removable under safe conditions.

Compliance References: Compliance with WHS Regulation 200 and AS 4024.1602 is essential to ensure safe access control and machine isolation.  and Plant Safety Requirements No. 1 ~ SHE-MSP-WHS-07.013

Collision Sensor

Inspection Criteria: Collision sensors must be securely mounted, and functioning as intended. These sensors must reliably detect proximity to obstacles or personnel and trigger appropriate alerts or automatic shutdowns to prevent accidents. They must operate independently of other control systems and remain effective under varying environmental conditions.

Compliance References: Installation must comply with WHS Regulation 191, AS 4024 series standards, and Plant Safety Requirements No. 1 ~ SHE-MSP-WHS-07.013. Inspection procedures should confirm sensor responsiveness, coverage area, and integration with emergency systems.
